Hey there. I just replied to someone about the EFA cards: http://forum.gbadev.org/viewtopic.php?t=9921

And to answer your questions:
1. Yes! Very.
2. Yes! Among all other save memory types, clock, and has fast NOR mem.
3. Yes, it's Megabits. Since 256Mib is the largest block a GBA can address I recommend that one. Flashcarts that have more than 256Mib use a bank switching technique to access the rest. (Unrelated mention, I don't think any games for the GBA are larger than 256MiB. If there are, they are few.) I bought a 512Mib when they came out, but it was pointless and I sort of regretted wasting the extra money on it.
4. It works on every GBA revision and DS revision.

(I feel like a salesperson here... but I'm really quite happy with mine so I do recommend it.)

#90393 - dantheman - Fri Jun 30, 2006 2:39 am

Carts come in sizes larger than 256 megabits because people like having more space for their stuff. With larger carts, you can have more programs on there at once, which means less reflashing. However, you stated that you'll simply take some things off to add the new ones, so it doesn't make that big of a difference.

The only times I really use the full amount of space on my 1 gigabit cart is when I'm adding movies to it. Those take up a lot of space for relatively short video segments. Otherwise, my cart is generally about half full, mostly with music using Tepples's GBA GSM player and the occasional game or two.
