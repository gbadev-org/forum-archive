#88477 - tepples - Mon Jun 19, 2006 6:21 pm

The GBAMP SD firmware isn't even dumped yet. We need somebody with a GBA (classic or SP, not a Micro or DS), a GBAMP SD, and a GBA serial cable (MBV2 or Xboo), but nobody in the gbadev.org crowd or the PIRACY-DOT-COM crowd seems to have all three parts.

The plan:
  1. Dump the GBAMP SD firmware.
  2. Run the firmware in a modified GBA emulator that logs all Game Pak I/O.
  3. Find where in the firmware the sector driver lives and disassemble it.
  4. Figure out the algorithm that the GBAMP SD uses to read or write a sector.
  5. Figure out the firmware unlock sequence.
  6. Reimplement this algorithm into Chishm's FAT driver.
  7. Recompile NDSMP against the new FAT driver.


Moved to the GBA Flash Equipment forum because we need to reach out to people with GBA hardware.
