I'm using a Flash2Advance USB on Win98. I've found that either the USB Linker or Windows USB support is rather flakey.

If I leave the linker plugged into my computer all the time, then eventually Windows will stop recognizing the device. I'll try using it, and the software will report that the linker couldn't be found.

When that happens, if I unplug the linker, I'll get a blue screen. The system won't be very stable, so I reboot. If I wait until after the boot finishes to plug the cable back in, it will be detected fine for a while, until the cycle repeats. Plugging the cable back in before rebooting will just lead to another blue screen, and it still won't work.

So, if you leave your linker plugged in all the time, that may be the problem.
