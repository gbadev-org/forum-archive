#80169 - tepples - Wed Apr 19, 2006 2:01 pm

Play-Yan has an onboard MP3 audio and MPEG-4 video decoder. However, it can't be used for homebrew. If you don't want to buy both a Play-Yan and something else, you'll probably want a SuperCard CF so that you can run GSM Player, which has acceptable quality over, say, street noise.

#80176 - sumiguchi - Wed Apr 19, 2006 3:15 pm

I have Supercard SD and use it for playing audio - but am using the Movie Player software - so you need to convert MP3's to its format.

(You can download the software and try it out if you like from http://www.supercard.cn)

With a play-yan adapter, it comes with its own audio out - so you get 16 bit sound which is better quality than using the jack on the micro - which is 8 bit out.

Personally, the audio quality of supercard/movie player is fine for me...on the bus, train etc....
