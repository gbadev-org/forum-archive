Yes. It means you can start homebrew with one piece of hardware, even if you don't gain resistance to (rare) brickers.

Quote:
Also, would I need a slot 2 device as well?

Yes; otherwise, you won't be able to run GBA homebrew.

#134781 - dantheman - Mon Jul 16, 2007 6:38 pm

You don't -need- a slot-2 device, no, but like Tepples said, that's the only way to gain GBA compatibility. A popular option is to get a slot-1 device like the R4 combined with the slot-2 EZ-V 3in1 expansion pack, which gives you GBA functionality for about $20 extra.
