Many games for 8- and 16-bit platforms had nine pre-rendered sprite tiles for 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, and 8 pixels full. I know at least Tet—èis (by Tengen) had all 8 for its tetromino frequency display, and the Mega Man series used states 0, 2, 4, 6, and 8. Games would either load all nine tiles into sprite VRAM and then use OAM to select one, or they would load empty, full, and middle cels and overwrite the middle tile with partially full tile data as needed.
