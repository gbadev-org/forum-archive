#37554 - tepples - Sun Mar 13, 2005 1:58 am

"Surround sound" on a stereo signal can mean one of two things:
  • Playing a sound effect 180 degrees out of phase when it's panned behind the listener. This is called matrix surround, which Dolby Pro Logic receivers can decode into a passable approximation of 3-channel surround.
  • Feeding a delayed, attenuated, low-pass filtered version of one speaker's signal to the other speaker. Such a "headphone compensation" circuit simulates the behavior of a pair of speakers aimed at a head.
