#31728 - Alex Atkin UK - Tue Dec 14, 2004 4:11 am

Lets look at it from the most logical point of view.

The GBA had a little button that was pressed when a GB/GBC cart was installed. This switched it into GBC mode which not only switched the BIOS over and the CPU but also output a higher voltage to the cartridge port than what GBA games use.

I find it unlikely the DS is designed to handle greater than 3.3V to the GBA port as why would it need more? So by that logic alone, it doesnt have the power to run a GB cart.

If it COULD do it so easily then there would be no point in Nintendo not including that functionality as standard as it would be a selling point. The only logical reason NOT to include it is to save money, meaning it just cant do it period.

Of course it will be able to emulate a GB, even the GBA could do that, but the DS being a lot faster should plausably be able to emulate a GBC (and plenty of other old consoles) perfectly. This still relies on a flash cart though so we are still waiting on progress for that.

Considering how long people have had their hands on DS hardware things are coming along quite well.
