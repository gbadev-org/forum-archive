#99086 - Cearn - Sat Aug 19, 2006 2:49 pm

I'd love to start writing for the DS. However, there are two major issues that keep me from doing so at the moment.

The first one is time. I'm in the process of updating pretty much everything I have right now which, well, takes a while. Tonc in particular needs across the board changes, in particular to make sure its codebase can work nicely with libgba. It's dumb, time-consuming work, but it needs to be done before I can move on.

The second is more DS related. I only got my DSLite a couple of weeks ago. As far as I know the emulators aren't stable enough for real development (though it seems no$gba is getting there fast), so there's hasn't really been a way to even get started. And then there's the insane about of hardware options, with potential incompatibilities between them -- I'm not particularly looking forward to browse through the dozens of threads to come to a shopping list for a non-flashing DSLite solution (and yes, that is a hint :P).
