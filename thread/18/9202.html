#79385 - Two9A - Thu Apr 13, 2006 10:18 am

Ok, the logical fallacy here is that the DS is capable of playing XviD at normal speed. It simply isn't. Moonshell struggles with MPEG-1, although admittedly it hasn't been optimised for all the variables that can be hardcoded (256x192, 4:2:0, 16-bit output, and so on).

MPEG-4 is a lot more complicated, which is one reason why small iterations of the encoding requirements can produce entirely different codecs. It also takes a lot more power to decode. There's a reason why my little Pentium-233 can't decode XviD to save its life, but does MPEG-1 fullscreen just fine.

Overclocking your DS isn't gonna help. Not unless you can run it at 500% normal speed, and that would mean liquid helium. The LCD wouldn't like that.
