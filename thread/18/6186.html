#46905 - tepples - Fri Jul 01, 2005 10:52 pm

A "16-bit 65C02" is a 65C802, which in turn is a 65C816 without the A16-A23 address lines. The 65C802 might even be pin compatible with 65C02. Or a "16-bit 65C02" could be another customized 6502 derivative such as the SPC700, which has instructions that treat X and Y as a 16-bit number, except without the SPC's "just different enough to avoid a lawsuit" instruction encoding.
