You can't just replace the ARM9 in the Nintendo DS because the ARM9 is embedded in a microcontroller that also contains the 2D and 3D graphics cores, the sound core, and all sorts of other I/O. It's not like the Sega Genesis, where all I/O is on an ASIC separate from a stock Freescale MC68000 CPU.
