#94974 - tepples - Thu Jul 27, 2006 3:49 am

thegamefreak0134 wrote:
I understand this. Thing is, I purchaced a copy.

But you didn't purchase a license to have additional copies made. Only the music publisher can grant such a license. Likewise, just because you own a copy of Super Mario Bros. doesn't mean you can transcribe the maps into your own homebrew game. I too own a lawfully made copy of this CD by Hampton the Hampster, but that doesn't mean I can distribute my own S3M cover version willy-nilly.

Quote:
I just want to know if a good s3m format version of it exists.

It can exist if you learn to use Modplug Tracker.

Quote:
I don't actually intend to use the whole thing, just chunks of it.

Using a "significant" chunk (as defined in case law) without authorization is just as much an infringement as using the whole thing.
