Help Wanted > Land of Relics in Need of Secondary Artist

#12402 - Matto154 - Tue Nov 11, 2003 4:00 pm

I am currently seeking a pixel artist to help along the development of our RPG project, "Land of Relics", for the Gameboy Advance. The work required is pretty general, sprites, backgrounds, etc. Pretty much whatever you feel like working on. The project was started a little over a year ago, by me and my friend. In that time, we have come quite a long way. Our group currently consists of:

1 programmer
1 writer
1 artist
1 music composer
1 sound engineer

I am currently acting as lead artist, and have tried desperately to handle all the artwork involved. I have set the general sprite style and tileset style. However, there is just so much work that needs to be done, including game design, which I have taken upon myself to work on. I am currently working on promotional art as well, so that we can get the website launched for the game. In the meantime, our programmer is waiting patiently for more pixel art to be sent his way so he can work on the engine a bit more.

As far as the story goes, the writer is very knowing in the ways of writing and drama. The script is still a little sketchy at this point, but it is coming along nicely. It mainly revolves around themes of frailty among human kind. It is roughly 40 pages right now.

Our musician is heavily inspired by Final Fantasy, and has composed a LOT of music over the years. You can find his work at http://www.gamemusic.ca.

Our programmer is a recent graduate of NYU, with a bachelors degree in programming. He completed a Gameboy Advance demo for his college thesis. He currently works on the Harvard University school of law website for work, and takes on several other projects on the side. Well, you know him, Codered, he posts here every so often.

At this point, you may be wondering what kind of benefits you will recieve. Honestly, I'm going to say this right now; there is no salary on this project. Yes, you will recieve royalties if we have this published, but it is a long way from being done. If we all work hard, it is certainly possible. But in the meantime, we are desperately saving money to pay for our websites, and to eventually register the company. Not to mention all the costs of licensing and all that. But yes, if you perform well, and you truly contribute to the project, you will be paid in the end; no doubt about that. And let it be known right now, I am not looking for someone to work FOR me, I'm looking for someone to work WITH me.

If anyone feels they are experienced enough to undertake this position, please contact me at matt@divinus.net for more details. Attaching sample work on your email is a plus.

Our "company" website is located at http://www.divinus.net. The official "Land of Relics" website is http://www.landofrelics.com. No, we're not a registered business or anything yet, we're actually saving up money to do that, god knows Nintendo would never grant a license to someone who doesn't even have a registered business. But yes, I do know all the risks involved and all that, I've spent over a year researching the game development business and all that.
