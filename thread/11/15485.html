"The dude" probably told you to join the forum and *learn* to program for the NDS (or in general)... or to find programmers that are interested in your projects, but sure not to ask people to do the dirty work for you.

There is a difference between searching peers for a project and asking people to do things for you. Again, if nobody finds your idea interesting enough, you have to move on, until you either know how to realize your ideas yourself or you come up with an idea that others will like and help you realize. But either way, there will always be a lot of work on your side and people need to see that you are willing to do most of the work yourself, if that's programming or art or anything else. Nobody will work for you, but with you and if people see that you ask for things, so that you can tell everyone it's "your" demo/mod/whatever, although you did virtually nothing for it, you're standing pritty much alone.

There is no such thing as NDS editing. You can modify the hardware, but I guess that's not what you want. "Creating" for the NDS would be a better term. If you're just editing/moding DS DooM, that's not NDS specific. There are aspects of the port, that *are* platform specific, but I rather doubt, that you touch any of it.

My advise, again, is: Look at some general programming tutorials, then take the NDS examples and work with those until you are familiar enough to realize your ideas by yourself.
If you're not willing to take that years-long trip, get another hobby. That's my honest advise.
