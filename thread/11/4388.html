#29165 - Cyberman - Fri Nov 12, 2004 6:15 pm

Well the biggest problem is all those names are likely trademarked.

You'll get sued if you use existing trademarked names. Trademarks are much more vigorously defended than copyrights. This prevents a Chinese company from producing undiserable merchandise with Hulk Hogan's name on it for example, AND not paying for the use of the name. Apart form the legalese situation there.. mmm this whole thread is a bit silly (like Camalot in Monty Python's holy grail).

To answer the original poster, you're asking someone to make a game for you? Have you any idea how much work goes into these games? (The comercial ones are just fun enough to play most times, I enjoy the RPG's since they at least make me think). Try taking a year out of your life and do nothing but think about "World Wildlife Federation" (I have to admit it's a clever abuse of wwf) and a game related to it, programing artwork audio and all. Consider 10 hour days and sometimes 6 day work weeks with no reward (or pay or food water or any of life's necessitys to live) other than someone thanking you for the game. That's reality. Ideas are common as dirt, implementors are few.
