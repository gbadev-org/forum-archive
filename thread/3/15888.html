#164323 - Pukys - Tue Oct 28, 2008 5:45 pm

GrizzlyAdams wrote:
in other words it probably adjusts the voltages going to the lcd, and playing with it is likely to kill the lcd.


I don't think, it'll kill the LCD. Most likely, it is to fine-tune Vcom, that is used for compensation on the LCD. If it is incorrectly set, there will become a line-structure visible on the display, alongside with a significant loss of contrast (till the screen turns blank). But, this is normally no way to kill a display, because the loss of display quality is usually reversible (just fine-tune, till the quality looks good).
Alas, if the adjustment is meant for the backlight of the display, it actually could kill the whole thing, but this is not very likely: CCFL inverters on this scale usually don't need external regulation.
