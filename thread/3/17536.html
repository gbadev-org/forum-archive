#176293 - Dwedit - Thu Jun 16, 2011 4:59 am

I once had problems with my GBA SP's shoulder buttons, and I was somehow able to fix them.

Normally, the button has a little rubber thing covering up a hole, then there's a flat plastic button switch inside there. I noticed that I was able to get the button to work when I poked at the switch with a pin. So I just stuck some "Stuff" in there (I used a crumpled up wad of aluminum foil), then put the little rubber thing back in there, then it miraculously worked. This problem was caused by the button assembly not pushing hard enough on the actual switch.

But not all shoulder button problems are the same, your mileage may vary.
