#76593 - tepples - Thu Mar 23, 2006 12:30 am

Pet peeve: There is no "VirtualboyAdvance". There is "Virtual Game Boy Advance", an obsolete emulator by Marat, and then there is "VisualBoyAdvance", a more current emulator by Forgotten and kxu. I'll assume you mean the latter.

Usually if you see "unimplemented BIOS function 0x0d", you can safely ignore it. It just runs a checksum on the BIOS. Many programs use this to detect GBA vs. Nintendo DS vs. emulators so that they can work around emulator bugs or use a less-brightened palette on the DS's brighter screen.
