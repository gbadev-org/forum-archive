Good question. It'd be worth pushing out a new version of the gba version with the minor map changes, which I might do if I ever get around to it. (The most significant map-related change was because there was a problem causing the switch to open the door to the Attack Ring in the lake dungeon to very occasionally not appear)

Most of the other bug fixes, interestingly enough, didn't actually manifest themselves in problematic ways on the gba, (there was a place where I was dereferencing an uninitialized pointer -- on the DS, it totally crashed everything. On the GBA, it didn't cause problems (I believe Wintermute suggested that this difference in behavior was a purposeful change in the toolchain)), so I most likely won't mess with backporting any of those fixes to the GBA. (since I'm using a separate source tree for the different versions)
