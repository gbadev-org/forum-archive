#118118 - dantheman - Sat Feb 10, 2007 6:15 am

DHCP usually doesn't work too well in homebrew, so you're going to have to figure out the static settings. I would recommend you go into your official WFC settings and change it from Automatic to manual. You can usually find the correct info either from your router's administration page or by typing "ipconfig" at a command prompt. The IP can be anything you like as long as it's within your network. For instance, my PC is 192.168.1.100, so for my DS's IP I chose 192.168.1.125. Subnet Mask you can find on your admin page (mine is 255.255.255.0), and Gateway is your router's IP address.

DNS servers can usually be found on your router's administration page, though sometimes it might work if you just type in the IP address of your router itself. It works for me at least, though I generally prefer to use the actual DNS IPs.

Alternatively, if you have a new DS Lite, you might not be able to connect at all due to a change in the Wifi hardware. DSAIM would need to be recompiled with the new WIFI library to fix this.
