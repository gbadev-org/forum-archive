RSS and Atom are both ways of getting information, such as news, from a site. Take www.drunkencoders.com for example. They have an RSS feed, so if you entered that RSS feed's URL into ndsmail, it would download all of the news items from drunkencoders. If you keep that feed, then whenever you clicked retrieve (or whatever the button is), it would download all of the new news items from the site. So, in summary, it's just an easy way of getting news from a site.

SSL is a way of securely transmitting data over the internet. Since it's "encrypted", so to speak, it's a bit more sophisticated then normal internet traffic and thus it's not really supported by the current wifi library for the DS. Since gmail requires you to use SSL to connect to their email servers, you can't access your gmail account through ndsmail. I don't think this is compatible with yahoo mail, unless you can find a way to access your yahoo mail through a POP server, which yahoo disables for their free accounts, i believe.

As for online content, just about anything you could ever want to know about has an RSS feed or an Atom feed (note that the two terms aren't interchangeable). To sign up for it, all you do is enter the URL of the feed into a feeder, such as ndsmail.

I hope this helps to answer some of your questions!
