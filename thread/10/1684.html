#8548 - tepples - Sun Jul 13, 2003 9:33 pm

To rip the sounds, get a program that can read raw binary files. Most games store their samples in 8-bit signed format; some store samples in 8-bit unsigned format (which enables a minor mixer speedup); a few (such as Super Puzzle Fighter II) compress their samples because there are so darn many of them to fit in 8 megabytes.

To rip the music? You'll have to reverse engineer the music engine, the same thing that NSF and GBS ripping experts do.
