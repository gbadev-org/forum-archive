#82374 - tepples - Fri May 05, 2006 8:33 pm

There are multiple at-like signs in Unicode:
  • @ (U+0040 commercial at)
  • (a) (U+0028 left parenthesis, U+0061 latin small letter a, U+0029 right parenthesis)
  • ? (U+00A4 currency sign)
  • (α) (U+0028 left parenthesis, U+03b1 greek small letter a, U+0029 right parenthesis)
  • (а) (U+0028 left parenthesis, U+0430 cyrillic small letter a, U+0029 right parenthesis)
  • parentheses around any of several accented a's in Latin, Greek, or Cyrillic
  • Ѧ (U+0466 cyrillic capital letter little yus) (looks like an A and T combined)
  • ⒜ (U+249C parenthesized latin small letter a)
  • Ⓐ (U+24B6 circled latin capital letter a)
  • ⓐ (U+24D0 circled latin small letter a)
  • ♭ (U+266D music flat sign) (Flat rhymes with At)
  • ㋐ (circled katakana a)

And lots of dots:
  • * (U+002A asterisk)
  • . (U+002E full stop)
  • ? (U+00B7 middle dot)
  • ? (U+2022 bullet)
  • ∙ (U+2219 bullet operator)
  • ■ (U+25A0 black square)
  • ▪ (U+25AA black small square)
  • ● (U+25CF black circle)
  • ◦ (U+25E6 white bullet)
  • ☉ (U+2609 sun)
  • 。 (U+3002 ideographic full stop)
  • ・ (U+30FB katakana middle dot)
  • ． (U+FF03 fullwidth full stop)
  • ･ (U+FF65 halfwidth katakana middle dot)

Vary which at-sign and dot-sign you use, and spammers will have to make their spam tools Unicode-capable.
