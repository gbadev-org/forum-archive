#27687 - Joat - Tue Oct 19, 2004 4:03 am

I ordered an EZFA 256 a week ago Sunday and it shipped last Tuesday. It arrived some time between Thursday and Saturday (was out of town).

I'd certainly reccomend Jandaman to others, especially because of the cheap shipping to US addresses ($2.50 for USPS).

A word of caution to people about the EZFA (nothing to do with Jandaman): They used a USB interface chip made by FTDI, and did *not* change the default USB ID code, so it gets a driver auto-installed by windows XP if you don't have any other FTDI devices installed (the installation instructions at Jandaman cover this situation), but if you own or build other devices using FTDI chips (which really are quite nice), you're in a spot of trouble, since the EZFA drivers and the standard serial driver cannot coexist (all for the sake of the idiots changing one value in the EEPROM)!

I'll be looking into how to get around this (in case it affects anyone else), but I'm not using any of my projects that use a FTDI chip on a regular basis, so its a low priority right now.
