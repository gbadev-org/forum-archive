#100688 - StoneCypher - Tue Aug 29, 2006 7:32 pm

It depends a lot on the nature of the project. If the publisher is funding development, then you almost always have to turn over sourcecode regularly as part of the milestone process, so that the publisher knows that things are proceeding and that the product is of high quality.

However, if you sell a game that's already completed, it's very rarely an issue. Granted, that case is rarer in console than PC development, but if you get a publisher to just up and purchase a game after an evaluation, they rarely even ask for source, and it's essentially never a sticking point. This has been a big deal for me in the past, because I have certain technologies that I want to keep private.
