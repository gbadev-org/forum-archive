keldon wrote:
If you have a dynamic IP (i.e. you don't have a static one) then unplug your modem for about half a minute. Might work, and change your name too.


Probably won't work as most DHCP servers retain your lease with that MAC address.. You'd either have to change your MAC address or wait till the lease expires before you would receive a new IP from the DHCP server. You can usually check the length of your lease either on the router or if you are direct connected, ipconfig /all for windows. Most routers allow you to change your MAC, so it'd be easier just to change it for a few days (pick whatever you like :) ) and then change it back if you really care what your MAC is.
