#176172 - Quirky - Fri Apr 29, 2011 3:02 pm

I've been dabbling in the world of Android. And what a painful world it is! I didn't know how good we had it on the GBA and DS - where it's "write once, run anywhere" (on any DS at least).

On Android it seems it's "write once, run only on the one device you happen to have tested", on all the other devices you'll get crashes and weird bugs. And that's not even talking about API versions, I just use lowest-common-denominator Open GL ES 1.1 and Android 1.6.

My "favourite" review so far:

Quote:
Black screen lock-up on starting. Had to remove battery to recover. Not good. Desire.


... Desire there refers to the phone model, an HTC Desire. I've seen the game in question running perfectly on a guy at work's HTC Desire, yet here it causes the phone to lock up. It's madness. At least it's a more or less open and hackable platform, I suppose.
