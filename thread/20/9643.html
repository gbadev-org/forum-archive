#85067 - tepples - Fri May 26, 2006 9:37 pm

To whoever wants to work on a patch for MoonShell's text reader, here's how to implement simple word break for Latin, Greek, Cyrillic, and Japanese scripts.

In the scanning loop:
When it sees a newline character ('\r' or '\n'), it draws the line so far.
When it sees a space character (' ' or '\t'), it stores its position in a variable called lastSpace.
When it sees a hiragana followed by a kanji or katakana, it stores the hiragana's position in lastSpace. (This will break Japanese words after their case endings.)
When it has enough characters to fill the line, it draws everything up to lastSpace unless there wasn't a space in this line, in which case it draws all the characters in the line so far.

#90968 - mntorankusu - Mon Jul 03, 2006 7:32 pm

jbullfrog wrote:
mntorankusu wrote:
Make sure you don't have the backlight timeout turned off in your global.ini.


How do you turn your backlight timeout on?

Modify this entry in the global.ini:

Code:
[BacklightTimeout]

WhenStandby=5
WhenPicture=0
WhenText=0
WhenSound=5


The clock comes on when the backlights would normally be disabled from the timeout. When one of these values is set to zero, the clock will never come up in that situation, and the backlights will never go off.
