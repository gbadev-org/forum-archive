#69131 - Plat Sajack - Sat Jan 28, 2006 6:12 pm

What you speak of was and still is possible on the Sega Dreamcast. A few years ago, while learning about the Dreamcasts emulation capabilities, I came across a document that explained clearly, the process of taking a lightweight RPG2k game and modifying it's contents so that it would work seamlessly on the dreamcast. It worked. I honestly doubt you'll be able to find this document, as this was back in 2001 and the domain that hosted this amazing document no longer exists. I believe it was tellah[dot]net or something, I really can't remember. Maybe some DC guru's[GPFError, I know you remember this document, alot of people on dcemulation's boards knew about this]could poke at my memory. On the up side, however, I printed this document out and I know I have it... I just have to find it. Is this possible on the Nintendo DS? Please, don't make me laugh - I'm drinking koolaid at the moment and I don't desire to decorate my keyboard and mice with this blubbery coloring...
If I remember correctly, RPG Maker 2k has these things called Events: process that run concurrently alongside each other, eating up massive amounts of ram, so if some genius figured out a way to emulate the rpg2k games and their runtime requirements, that genius probably wouldn't have enough RAM to work with on DS to begin with.

Moreover, I think an RPGmaker from the ground up would be nice on the DS, of course. The DS's touch screen and microphone alone would validate any consideration that game development studios might have about such a project. Using the stylus, one would be without the need for a mouse. Using the microphone, one could record custom battle or fanfare sounds or voices and have them saved to a GBA option pack. In short, an RPGMaker would be great on the DS, IMHO.
