#100466 - Mr. Picklesworth - Mon Aug 28, 2006 2:47 am

Myst is the only one of the Myst games I didn't get through, sadly.
I loved all of them; Myst 4 especially... and Riven was in a class of its own; I just like how the puzzles somehow worked as the player trying to learn about a civilization instead of just trying to work around mehanisms built by some idiot with a lot of time on his hands.
It's a shame how different Myst 5 was... though it was still pretty good, I suppose, if I forget how well the previous developers did. (Even more of a travesty that the developer to make that imperfect finale was Cyan themselves). Still, it did have that nice animation work... especially with the faces. And the ending wasn't bad except for the disappointingly ugly view of Releeshahn. I don't see why they couldn't do FMV characters as before, since all the cutscenes had the game take control of the camera.

I have to play the original Myst some day, and it will have to be on the DS or some other small thing because playing stuff at low resolutions on my PC is just unbearable.

Riven would be great, too. Not sure if the DS could handle that one, but it could be possible...
The only problem in that case is storage space.

I'm surprised Cyan hasn't released their engine for those games. Presumably it's a little program that reads from a data file, and there's probably nothing totally revolutionary in the program itself.
They could get completely legal unofficial ports to all sorts of platforms, and still make money from people buying the data files!
