#91213 - Stonebone - Wed Jul 05, 2006 10:15 am

tepples wrote:
And how would garbage work in 4-player TA? I seem to remember that the Super NES and Nintendo 64 versions supported only two players.

The Gamecube version of Panel de Pon (found in Nintendo Puzzle Collection) has 4-player support and it's very good, though not as good as 2-player.

The color of the blocks in the last step of a chain determines who gets the garbage. So if the last step consists of hearts, the red player gets the garbage. The problem is that YOU could be the red player and you end up sending garbage to yourself, so you need to watch out.

In the case of a combo, the garbage is sent to each player with corresponding colors.

If no player is playing the particular color the garbage is sent to a random player other than you.
