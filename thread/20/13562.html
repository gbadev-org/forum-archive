#133163 - tepples - Tue Jul 03, 2007 10:15 pm

Here's how Lick did it:

German and Netherlandish are closely related languages; here, I use the regular expression De?uts?ch which matches both "Deutsch" and "Dutch". In these languages, the verb goes at the end of the sentence (like in Japanese) unless it's the first word of a verb in the main clause, in which case it goes after the first noun phrase or adverb phrase in statements or first in questions. In English, on the other hand, the verb goes after the subject noun phrase, except in questions where it goes first.
With this rearrangement, bitking wrote:
Hi, i search HACK drivers for wifimax (120.11b adapter) that can homebrew games and beup.
[...]
Wifimax working good by my, but i can not playing homebrew games on ds with wifimax. Maybe downloaden HACK drivers such as the hack drivers for de nintendo wifi stick...
Or to be there none HACK drivers for the wifimax?


"Search" and "seek" are the same in De?uts?ch. "Can", "can do", and "know" are the same. "No x" and "none of x" are the same. The De?uts?ch infinitive "X-en" corresponds to English "to X", but it is also the ending for plural present. The words for "the" in Dutch are "de" and "het", while German has a bunch more D-words all meaning "the". And some of the rules for pronoun case (I, me, my) differ from those in English.
With this vocabulary change, bitking wrote:
Hi, i seek HACK drivers for wifimax (120.11b adapter) that can do homebrew games and beup.
[...]
Wifimax working good by me, but i can not play homebrew games on ds with wifimax. Maybe to download HACK drivers such as the hack drivers for the nintendo wifi stick...
Or are there no HACK drivers for the wifimax?


Knowing a bit of Japanese grammar helps you understand Engrish. Likewise, in this case, knowing a bit of De?uts?ch grammar helps you understand Denglish.
