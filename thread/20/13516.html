#132332 - Cydrak - Mon Jun 25, 2007 11:08 pm

If you thought it was "nearly" impossible, why are you asking if anyone else will do it? Is there a salary or lots of pizza involved? ;)

If it's worth that much to you, there are already excellent, free tools and resources out there. And mind, I had PC experience first, but I've -really- enjoyed learning the DS. :-) Anyway, I've thought about porting stuff, and often, you have to ask yourself some genuinely hard questions.

First of all the DS screens are 256x192. Those are each *1/16* of the PC screenshots, and smaller fonts aren't gonna get you all the way there. Do you think the maps/radar will be legible? How much needs to be seen at once? Do some mockups in Gimp or Paint, to give yourself an idea.

Is chat an important part of the game? Especially during battle? How do you handle text input? The DS would prolly do better with voice chat, if you had a fast enough codec...

72mb is quite a lot for the DS. And that's just the compressed installer. Probably the bulk is graphics, which will shrink 4-16x to fit the smaller screen. If you're lucky it will be more like 16mb, and it will need a FAT device, maybe one with extra RAM.

It's a cute little handheld with a mean punch, but it can't do everything.
