#97605 - JaJa - Thu Aug 10, 2006 7:16 pm

Ah, abandonware.
Another great urban legand.

"What do you mean I have to pay for it, it's 11 years old, they can't make any more money out of it!"

Wikipedia:
"Under copyright law, copyright owners have the right to profit or not profit from their creations; there is no requirement for a copyright holder to use the copyright in commerce."

So, until the copyright expires (some 70 to 120 years) it's still illegal whether they can make money from it or not.

Also even though the original publisher/creator may have gone or stopped selling the software, other companies may have bought the rights, like many Atari games.

That's not to say all games are protected, some companies have given permission (rather than it just being assumed due to the age) for the game to be given away, and in some cases even released the source code for it.

For more information see Wikipedia
