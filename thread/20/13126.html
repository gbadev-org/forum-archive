I know Nintendo's warranty is a year after purchase, but they will still repair it afterwards (thought you will likely have to pay for the repair). They don't care if you include the dust cover thing or not, and they told me that irregardless of what firmware is in it, unless you opened up the system the warranty is still standing.

This saved me once as I screwed my first DS Lite up totally trying to flash it. I really should have tried to insulate the screwdriver first. >_> It very obviously didn't have the stock firmware, being the non-stealth version of Flashme (battery cover wouldn't come off, the screw was stuck so I couldn't re-flash it), but they still took it in.

This Curry's...I have no idea, it's probably up to whatever their return policy is. If you can't return it there you are probably best off calling Nintendo...more than likely they will send you a new DS Lite and the stuff to send your broken one back to them.
