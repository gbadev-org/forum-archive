#56274 - tepples - Fri Oct 07, 2005 3:45 pm

An audio decoder running at CD spec has to decode 44100*2 = 88,200 signal points per second. A video decoder running at full screen 24fps has to decode 256*192*24 = 1,179,648 signal points per second in addition to the audio. It's difficult to get a decoder optimized that far, especially given that MPEG-4 video (DivX/XviD) is more computationally complex than MPEG-1 video. This is part of why Play-Yan uses an external decoder on the GBA cartridge and why GBA Video uses a much less complex codec.
