#102435 - Lynx - Thu Sep 14, 2006 1:20 am

Well, the reason I don't think it's helful at all is because Linux doesn't even have a navigation system. Sure, there are plenty of mapping softwares around, but none that will do actual navigation. I believe this is because of two reasons. Either A)encryption routines used by commercial navigation systems is stronger then they are willing to commit time to try to crack/they change to much or B)liscensing is to expensive for non-commercial developers.

So, even having vector maps isn't going to give you the routing information required to make decisions. If it's just maps you want, "borrow" them from any of the GPL GPS mapping applications already available.
