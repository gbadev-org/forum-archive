#60538 - M3d10n - Fri Nov 11, 2005 3:44 pm

It isn't confirmed, but seems the USB dongle only works with DS'es (you probably can't use it as a generic wifi card).

But it's possible to use any USB/PCI card (not only a router) to setup an ad-hoc connection. It's just that you must manually setup it to share the internet connection and do the DHCP work, using the manufacturer's software. Since the steps to setup such thing varies from card to card, Nintendo decided to keep quiet about it. They aren't saying it's unsupported either.

The dongle is also needed if your router is using WPA or WPA2, since stupid Nintendo didn't add support to it, and the DS will simply hit a brick wall trying to connect to such networks. You can plug the dongle to a computer connected to the WPA network, and have it create a DS-only access point.

My Email wrote:
Ok, so I'm not totally sure how the Nintendo USB thing works, but I think the main theory behind it is that it's pretty much the same as an Wireless PCI card or the Wireless USB dongle. What I think it does, is the same as what a computer can make with an "ad hoc" network, which is a direct link between two computers, but in the DS's case, The DS and the computer.

So in theory, could you use a Wireless PCI card and make a ad hoc connection between a PC and the DS?


Reply wrote:
That's a great question. As long as your Wi-Fi adapter is compatible with the 802.11b standard, it should work with the Nintendo Wi-Fi Connection; however, we have encountered some stability and/or compatibility issues with some routers that are compatible with 802.11b, so we can't know for sure at this point. What I would recommend is visiting your manufacture's web site and check for an online or PDF manual for your adapter. It might not address your specific question, but it may be useful to help you troubleshoot any problems you may encounter once you get your first Nintendo Wi-Fi Connection game and attempt to connect.
