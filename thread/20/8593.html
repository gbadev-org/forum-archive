#71987 - tepples - Wed Feb 15, 2006 9:20 pm

Two reasons:
  • Demodulators and decoders for digital terrestrial broadcast television are not yet small enough to fit in an Option Pak. Phone TV receiver hardware is designed to fit in a handheld handset from day one.
  • The 8VSB modulation used for ATSC television in the United States is very sensitive to the Doppler effect, which means that it's not suitable for the moving bus or train environments where the DS is often found. The TDMA-based modulation of GSM is designed to work from a moving handset from day one.
