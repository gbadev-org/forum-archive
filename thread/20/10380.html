#92988 - LS5 - Sun Jul 16, 2006 9:12 am

Slightly dissapointing because he doesn't mention how to actually get the FLV files themself, but nice nonetheless. The idea behind it is quite simple; install an FLV splitter so your media player can handle them, let FFDShow decode the video part, and let whatever MP3 decoder you're using decode the audio part (YouTube videos contain a 8kbps 22050Hz mono MP3). Oh, and this method works for BatchDPG too. :)
