#75794 - m2pt5 - Thu Mar 16, 2006 1:31 am

stan wrote:
(haven't tried unflashme, though... I wasn't sure if there was a confirmation request, and didn't want to reflash my DS)

It's the same XBXB confirmation as FlashMe. noflashme.nds flashes the unprotected portion first, then the protected portion. (Reverse of FlashMe.) You don't short for noflashme until about 70% or so.

If you run noflashme and stop/poweroff when it stops for you to short, you can still recover to flashme with the A+B+Select+Start emergency recovery code. (I had to do this once when I was messing around with noflashme, preparing it and flashme for flashing and possibly unflashing a couple friends' DSes. I mistakenly hit XBXB and bricked my DS. Because of this, I discovered that the emergency recovery code with a SupercardSD and latest SC firmware can actually boot to the SC menu.)
