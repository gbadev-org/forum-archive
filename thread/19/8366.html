#69693 - CrazyMike - Wed Feb 01, 2006 8:21 am

I'm posting this to help other that have this problem, since I found this problem in other forums and most of them did not have the answer. Incidentally, I found the basis for the solutuion at http://m3-forum.com/, although I had to do quite a bit of digging to find it.

The solution was to copy an m3 update to the sd card and (this part might be optional) the the BACKING directory. Specifically, I wiped the card(this might not be necessary), then I copied the updagcsd.e14 and BACKING directory to the card. After inserting everthing back into the ds, I then booted into gba mode (hold select down while turn the ds on and the select gba). I just followed the on screen instructions then.

The m3update is available at http://www.m3adapter.com/Download.htm.
