#87335 - Aisu - Tue Jun 13, 2006 1:33 pm

Ok so I got it and flashed it, everything is working perfectly. One thing to know is that you can still adjust the light levels NORMALLY AS IF YOU HAD AN UNFLASHED DS LITE!! So no worries guys. I tried out that drill bit and it totally wasn't connecting the SL1, so I just used one of those flat screwdrivers that are used for glasses. You can buy one of these virtually anywhere.

Honestly if you screw up flashing the DS Lite because you touched the screw on the right side of the SL1, you have to either
1) have really crappy hand eye coordination
2) be blind
3) totally be a noob and touch the screw instead of the SL1.

It is extremely easy to flash the US ds lite and there are no problems whatsoever. If anyone has any questions and wants pics or a video that can be arranged, just post and I will reply. [/b]

#87337 - Aisu - Tue Jun 13, 2006 1:39 pm

Just to remind you all that drill bit tool that I posted totally sucks, it did not make my ds lite go up in the flashing process by even 1%. Just use a flathead screwdriver that is used for tightening the screws on glasses, or get one of those mini PC flathead screwdrivers. ALSO KEEP IN MIND DO NOT BUY A SCREW DRIVER THAT HAS A MAGNETIC TIP THIS CAN DAMAGE ELECTRONIC COMPONENTS. The flashing process took less than a minute, it was surprisingly fast.

I just remembered something, to flash the ds lite with a passme2 you need to drag and drop the flashme installer and the SRAM file from the game that you will be putting in your passme2. SRAM files can be found on this website
http://darkfader.net/ds/games/
They are on the right hand side in the colum that says SRAM. Just download whichever sram for the game you have programed your passme2 device for, and you should be fine.
