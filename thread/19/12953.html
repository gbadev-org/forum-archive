#125033 - tepples - Wed Apr 11, 2007 12:13 am

Look at the back of your Nintendo DS. The DS Game Card slot is marked "SLOT-1", while the GBA Game Pak slot is marked "SLOT-2". The Game Pak slot on a Game Boy Advance, Game Boy Advance SP, Game Boy Player, or Game Boy micro corresponds to SLOT-2.

#125043 - HyperHacker - Wed Apr 11, 2007 12:31 am

You can get cards that fit into either slot to use homebrew with. Slot-1 cards are smaller and generally you just plug them in and go. Slot-2 cards require either a firmware hack or a slot-1 card to boot them (though these are much cheaper than standalone slot-1 cards and generally come with the device), and may stick out of the slot (especially with DS Lite), but may offer things such as standard SD card or Compact Flash support, GBA compatibility (for playing GBA games on the DS or using the card on a GBA), extra memory for homebrew to use, and/or rumble support.
