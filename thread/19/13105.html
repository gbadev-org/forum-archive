DS Flash Equipment > for moonshell video -- G6 or M3Simply?

#127245 - RavenWorks - Tue May 01, 2007 4:45 am

A friend of mine is looking for hardware to watch videos on his DS, and before I recommend something, I want to know if different hardware has significant differences in read speed (specifically, the decision is coming down between an M3 MicroSD slot-1 'simply', and the 4-gig G6 slot-2 flash device). I know the speed of the device is going to impose a limit on the fps/quality of the video; I read a review claiming the G6 couldn't get above 15 fps for videos in moonshell, does anyone have any experience that could confirm this?

#127246 - tepples - Tue May 01, 2007 5:05 am

R4 (same hardware as M3 DS Simply) runs my movies just fine. But then I don't use more than 15 fps so that I can use fullscreen and keep more video on a card. The animated cartoons that my little cousin likes to watch are drawn at 12 fps anyway.

A lot of the difference depends on the SD card. Some SD cards can read information faster than others. In theory, a SLOT-2 card with RAM (such as M3 Perfect or G6) might be faster than because it can copy an entire chapter of a film into a 32 MB RAM buffer on the card, but I don't think MoonShell supports this.
