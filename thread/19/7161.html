#57283 - Lynx - Fri Oct 14, 2005 8:39 pm

johnnywashngo wrote:
The problem I am having is that the NDS boots up in GBA mode and when I select the demo1.nds.gba

There is your first problem. You don't want to boot up in GBA Mode, and you need to rename the demo1.nds.gba file to _BOOT_MP.nds and put it on the ROOT of the CF card.

Or, get MightyMaxes multi-loader and put it on.. and use it to boot your demo1.nds.gba file.

Oh... now that I think about it, you should be getting two white screens. So, it sounds like the Super PassKey isn't redirecting? Is this a symptom of the new NDS firmware? So, I'd start by making sure your DS cart is plugged into the Super PassKey good, and make sure the Super PassKey is plugged into the NDS good.

#57307 - josath - Fri Oct 14, 2005 9:41 pm

Random notes & tips:

* Did you install the new GBAMP firmware that supports nds mode? (there are tutorials for this, one on http://ndshb.com i believe)

* As soon as you are in GBA mode, something has gone wrong. You need to be in NDS mode.

* If you have a passme/superpass in, and you boot into gba mode, that suggests there is something wrong with the superpass. At least with a passme, as long as it is plugged in, you will either get A) something running in nds mode, or B) two white screens.

* Also, you can NOT run .nds.gba files from gbamp (as far as i know). You need the .nds version of whatever you are trying to run.
