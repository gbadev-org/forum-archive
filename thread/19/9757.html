#85836 - grooverunner - Fri Jun 02, 2006 3:13 pm

Success!

Thanks for your help!

I managed to download flashme v7 from:

http://mobiles.gx-mod.com/modules/mydownloads/visit.php?lid=1700

I checked the MD5 against what bug suggested and it checked out. I even got the the "FLASHME INSTALLER (DS LITE)" screen chaRwie mentioned.

I cut off the end of a metal coat hanger to short SL1. I used a dremel tool to make the metal surface bare / clean. I insulated the sides of it by taping it up with some electrical tape. It was a perfect fit, didn't move around very much yet I could still spin it around if it wasn't making any contact. All up it took me about 2 minutes to get to 100% with some slight rotation every now and again to achieve contact. I made sure not to wiggle the wire around too much.

Hope this helps someone else hoping to flash an Aussie DS Lite.

Thanks again to bug and chaRwie for their suggestions.

#85838 - grooverunner - Fri Jun 02, 2006 3:36 pm

Ennead,

An MD5 check is something you do to ensure you have the correct/original file. It would be the equivalent of doing a fingerprint analysis to determine a person's identity.

I downloaded Flashme v7 from a French forum so I wanted to make sure it was the original thing and not something that would brick my DS. It turned out the be the correct file so I went ahead and installed Flashme v7.

I would suggest reading up on how to install Flashme on the DS Lite if you haven't done this sort of thing before. From what I understand it is very easy to short out other components on the DS Lite so it helps to be prepared.

I flashed two Australian NDS lite today with flashme v7 and zero problems.
I did get the warning text saying the firmware didn't match also. =)

It's so easy to flash it that I don't know how people could mess it up.
Just use a very small flathead screw driver with tape around the sides.
