#147963 - tepples - Mon Dec 31, 2007 4:50 am

To work around the 4 MB limitation, homebrew DS games are generally split into separate files: one .nds, and one or more other files that contain data loaded from the card at runtime. This is where "libfat" and "DLDI" come in, and kusma is right that the GnM works with these programs.

Bjohansen wrote:
Is there any homemade games which is a "must-have" ?

Must-have homebrew apps include DSOrganize (day planner+address book+IRC+web browser+text editor+music player+pic viewer), Colors! (paint), and MoonShell (music player+video player). Games popular among people who visit my house include MegaETk, Setsuzoku no Puzzle, and Lockjaw Tetromino Game ;-)
