#59553 - NoMis - Wed Nov 02, 2005 5:02 pm

I would go for a Movie Player, M3 or Supercard. They are all pretty good supported since all are supported by the FAT driver and allow access to the CF card. M3/Supercard are newer than the movie player but they also cost more. If you only want to use em for homebrew than there is nothing the Movieplayer can't do what the others can.

I would not go for a flashcard since the above mentioned adapters are generally not only cheaper but provide more features as well. You might also have other devices where you can use the CF card wich is another advantage over traditional flash cards.

You have 2 option to start Homebrew applications if you don't want to flash your firmware. You will need a passme device or a wireless PCI or PCMCIA card with the RT25xx chipset from Ralink to use wifime. You can find a complete list of devices that use the chipset here.
You can order passme from http://www.dspassme.com/ and you can get a movie player there as well.

I don't know however about the Firmware issues with new DS's. I think the Blue DS has a new firmware already so you will need passme2. I don't know if Wifime works with the new firmware. Theoretically it would not because it works the same way as the passme. Can someone who knows more about the firmware issue confirm that?

#59563 - Maverick - Wed Nov 02, 2005 6:18 pm

The current version of WiFiMe does not work with the newer firmware versions and i havent heard of anyone trying to make a new version, so for now, the only option if you have a new firmware would be to buy a passme and not a ralink wifi card. At least until you can flash your firmware.

If you dont want to flash your firmware because of the recent scare with mario kart and other TCP/IP wifi games, then you will need a passme.

I would recommend buying a GBA/MP and CF card to load your homebrew with a passme. Its cheap and it works.

Quote:
I don't know however about the Firmware issues with new DS's. I think the Blue DS has a new firmware already so you will need passme2.

There's blue, and then there's blue. Teal DS (Nintendogs bundle) = v4 firmware. Electric blue DS = new firmware only if you bought it in the last month or so.

WiFiMe does not work with v4 firmware, as the v4 firmware now checks the WMB header against more (presumably signed) parts of the .nds header. Somebody's going to have to actually crack SM64DS's protocol and use that to boot homebrew code.

#59618 - Lynx - Wed Nov 02, 2005 10:15 pm

If you bought your DS prior to October 2005, then the GBA MP and PassMe should be fine. If you bought it after October 1st, 2005, you may have new firmware (which, there doesn't seem to be a way to tell what version you have till you try a PassMe on it).. If you have new firmware, GBA MP isn't going to work, as PassMe2 requires SRAM. In that case, for the money, I think the SuperCard may be the next best option. But, as the prices climb, you have to do more personal reseach as to what exactly you want to be able to do with it.
