#108762 - MaHe - Sat Nov 11, 2006 11:14 pm

The answer is really simple, the only thing you have to decide is how much do you want to spend.

1. combination

- SuperKey
- SuperCard SD (make sure it's not Rumble version)

About 50$ altogether, assuming you can find a good store.

2. combination

- PassCard 3
- M3 Perfect SD Slim (make sure it's the Perfect, not Professional version)

About 80$ altogether, blahblah

The differences are small. SuperCard is made of cheaper parts and has ugly menus, but they're fast and functional. M3 on the other hand has a bit more quality, however (judging from the forums), techical issues are more likely (but still very rare). They both feature extra 32 megabytes of extra RAM, which could be used in the future. Instead of the SD versions, you can also spend about $20 more and buy microSD versions, so they won't stick out of your Lite - however, they won't accept the plain-sized SD cards and microSDs are a bit more expensive. The choice is all yours, but beware - SuperCard Lite still lacks some good homebrew support.

#108850 - Sektor - Sun Nov 12, 2006 11:34 am

For cheapest price and best compatiblity, you should get a GBA slot device.

Any slot-1 (DS slot) device that takes normal SD cards is going to stick out a few inches and reduce the portability of your DS. The NinjaDS is one of these cards but you are much better off getting a slot-2 (GBA slot) solution like Supercard or M3.

I think future official GBA expansions are unlikely and you wouldn't use them enough to make swapping too much of an inconvience.

#108867 - biohazard_star - Sun Nov 12, 2006 2:28 pm

You should get a Supercard SD and a Passme/Superkey. Do not get the Rumble series since they do not have the additional 32MB RAM. Flash your DS with Flashme afterwards so your DS can read NDS programs from the GBA slot.


Will any of those options allow for using a full sized SD card (inserted into DS1) with nothing in the GBA slot?

You can't insert an SD card into the DS card slot. If you're talking about DS card slot based flashcards, then there are ones available like NinjaDS and DS-Xtreme cards. These cards look exactly the same as normal NDS game cards. They allow you to read NDS/gba files from the DS slot, this means that you do not need to flash your DS anymore.
