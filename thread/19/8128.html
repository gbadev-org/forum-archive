#67070 - tetsujin - Sun Jan 15, 2006 8:28 am

I also considered buying a Linksys when I was shopping for an adaptor... I had no luck finding a rev. number on the box. I understand your frustration. I wound up buying an adaptor which I thought was OK according to the list (but in fact only certain rev.s of the adaptor had the RT2500 chipset, and the Ralink list didn't say which ones.) and had to return it.

I would suggest one of three routes.
1: Instead of buying at retail, buy an OEM card at a computer show. You'll be able to see the card itself, most likely, rather than marketroid packaging. (Returning if you screw up is more complicated, though...)
2: Buy online according to the shopping links on ralink.rapla.net, which supposedly point you at adaptors which have the chipset (caveat emptor...)
3: Stick to retail, but either be willing to abuse the return policy if it's the wrong chipset, or else buy a card that shows its rev. number on the packaging.

Me, I lucked out. I was shopping at Circuit City and I found a nice v3001 Belkin adaptor at a good price. Works like a charm.
