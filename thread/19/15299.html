#153557 - tepples - Tue Apr 01, 2008 10:10 pm

To run a homebrew .nds file, you need two files on a FAT or FAT32 formatted microSD card:
  • the R4 firmware (which comes on the CD or can be downloaded from the R4 manufacturer site)
  • the .nds file

R4 main menu has three buttons. From left to right:
  • Open a menu listing all folders and .nds files on the microSD card
  • Start MoonShell
  • Boot SLOT-2

To start a .nds file, you power on the DS, touch the left button (or press A), and navigate to the .nds file. Then you can run your Colors!, your Pocket Physics, your Setsuzoku no Puzzle, your DSOrganize birthday edition, or your Lockjaw Tetromino Game.

But there is a small defect in the R4's built-in DLDI file that affects some programs. So you may have to patch susceptible .nds files with the improved DLDI file and use DLDI No Auto Patch to hide the DLDI section from the R4's auto-patcher.
