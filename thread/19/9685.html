#84805 - zzo38computer - Wed May 24, 2006 9:23 pm

Upgrading the firmware is not risky if you can do it properly, but it will void your warranty. If the DS breaks, there is a way to fix it by opening it up, but first try flashing normally. You need a screwdriver or something to put into the SL1 hole (the hole in the battery compartment with a sticker covering it). Also, leave the battery in and plug into wall power as well. And do not turn it off until it says so, otherwise you will break it.

The firmware you can upgrade to are 2 choices I know of:
* FlashMe = the main menu will stay the same
* FWNITRO = the main menu will be different

http://www.dspassme.com/FlashMe.shtml Here you are. One more thing I should mention, if you download homebrew applications which include a .ds.gba file and a .nds file, you'll probably have to take the .ds.gba file, rename it to a .nds file, and upload that to your supercard. For example: dslinux.ds.gba -> dslinux.nds

The Supercard program is virus-free, and I'm fairly certain you don't need to use it for homebrew applications, at least I don't. Other things may require you to use it, however.
