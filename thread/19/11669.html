#108569 - tepples - Fri Nov 10, 2006 3:42 am

SuperCard and M3 can run full-size GBA homebrew. GBAMP v2 cannot.

SuperCard and M3 can take SD cards if you prefer to use those, and the homebrew support for SD cards will become stronger if and when wintermute manages to push out devkitPro r20. The GBAMP v2 SD, on the other hand, has not been cracked for use by homebrew file system code.

M3 has more GBA-mode compatibility than SuperCard.

#108571 - tepples - Fri Nov 10, 2006 3:52 am

carterman wrote:
1) How important is GBA-mode compatibility?

Without GBA-mode compatibility, you cannot play GBA homebrew. The GBAMP v2 can only run "multiboot" GBA homebrew such as LOCKJAW: The Overdose and the no-music version of Luminesweeper, not full-size GBA homebrew.

Quote:
2) How is compatibility with CF cards?

It's taken all three CF cards I've thrown at it (PQI, PNY, SanDisk). If it's your first time, I wouldn't recommend using a card that's too small (smaller than 128 MB) or too large (larger than 4 GB) or too thick (Microdrive).
