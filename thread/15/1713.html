#8700 - Quirky - Fri Jul 18, 2003 9:28 am

Depends on the style really. Arcadey football games should have their controls as simple as possible. One button for slide tackle, header, shoot and pass worked exceptionally well in Sensible Soccer - the console versions spoilt the balance by adding needless complexity IMO.

For "simulation" style footy games the controls can be more complex, providing it isn't just complexity for the sake of it. Winning Eleven, for example, has different pass types (through balls, one-twos, long passes), different tackling style (last gasp slide tackles/shoulder to shoulder pressure) allows you to sprint or just jog, etc etc.

I'd aim for a simple control scheme at first (1 or 2 buttons and limited features) and add things as you get a better idea of what you want to do.
